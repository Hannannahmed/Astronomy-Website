Stars


Stars are huge celestial bodies made mostly of hydrogen and helium that produce light and heat from the churning nuclear forges inside their cores. Aside from our sun, the dots of light we see in the sky are all light-years from Earth.

How many type of stars in the sky ?


Spectral Classification. There are seven main types of stars, and they are grouped by a system called spectral classification. This system organizes stars into groups by their temperature, color, and luminosity (brightness). These groups are the O, B, A, F, G, K, and M-class stars.


How many stars in the Sky ?


Using the Milky Way as our model, we can multiply the number of stars in a typical galaxy (100 billion) by the number of galaxies in the universe (2 trillion). The answer is an absolutely astounding number. There are approximately 200 billion trillion stars in the universe. Or, to put it another way, 200 sextillion.


How many galaxy stars ?


Astronomers had estimated that the observable universe has more than 100 billion galaxies. Our own Milky Way is home to around 300 billion stars, but it's not representative of galaxies in general.


Top brightest Stars

Sirius star

Sirius, also called Alpha Canis Majoris or the Dog Star. It is the brightest star in the night sky.


Canopus star

Canopus, also called Alpha carinae. The second-brightest star in the night sky.


Rigil Kentarus

It is also called Alpha Centauri. It is the brightest star in the night sky.


Arcturus star

Arcturus, also called Alpha Bootis. It is the third-brightest star in the night sky.


Vega star

Vega, also called Alpha lyrae. It is the brightest star in the northern constellation of Lyra .


Capella star

Capella, also called Alpha Aurigae. It is the brightest star in the sky.


Rigel star

Rigel, also called Beta Orinonis. It is a blue supergiant star in the constellation of Orion.


Procyon star

Procyon, also called Alpha Canis Manoris. It is the eighth-brightest star in the sky.
