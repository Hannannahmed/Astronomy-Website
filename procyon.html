What is Procyon Star?


Procyon is the brightest star in the constellation of Canis Minor and usually the eighth-brightest star in the night sky, with an apparent visual magnitude of 0.34. It has the Bayer designation α Canis Minoris, which is Latinized to Alpha Canis Minoris, and abbreviated α CMi or Alpha CMi, respectively.

Distance to Earth: 11.45 light years
Absolute magnitude: 2.68
Age: 1.701 billion years
Coordinates: RA 7h 39m 18s | Dec +5° 13′ 30″
Apparent magnitude (V): 0.4
Constellation: Canis Minor
Orbits: Galactic Center

Observational History


Procyon is the alpha star of the constellation Canis Minor the Small Dog. In mythology, Canis Minor is the smaller of two of Orion the Hunter's companion hunting dogs. The constellation Canis Major depicts the Great Dog, highlighted by Sirius, the sky's brightest star (after the sun). Procyon is a binary star system with a bright primary component, Procyon A, having an apparent magnitude of 0.34,[3] and a faint companion, Procyon B, at magnitude 10.7.[4]

What does the star Procyon mean?


Image result for what is procyon star before the dog The name Procyon is from Greek and means “before the dog,” a reference to the rising of this star shortly before Sirius, the larger Dog Star in the constellation Canis Major.

Which star is Procyon?


Procyon, also called Alpha Canis Minoris, brightest star in the northern constellation Canis Minor (Lesser Dog) and one of the brightest in the entire sky, with an apparent visual magnitude of 0.41.

Is Procyon hotter than the Sun?


Procyon B is a white dwarf star, confirmed since 1896. It is considerably smaller than Procyon A, at around 0.6 solar masses – 60% of the sun's mass. It is much hotter than our sun, and even Procyon A – at an average surface temperature of around 7.750 K
