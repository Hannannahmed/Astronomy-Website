CAPELLA STAR

Capella is the brightest star in the northern constellation of Auriga. It has the Bayer designation α Aurigae, which is Latinised to Alpha Aurigae

What is Capella Star?


Capella is the brightest star in the northern constellation of Auriga. It has the Bayer designation α Aurigae, which is Latinised to Alpha Aurigae and abbreviated Alpha Aur or α Aur.

Magnitude: 0.08
Absolute magnitude: -0.48
Distance to Earth: 42.92 light years
Constellation: Auriga
Coordinates: RA 5h 16m 41s | Dec +45° 59′ 53″
Spectral type: G3III:
Apparent magnitude (V): 0.08

Observational History


Observational history. Capella was the brightest star in the night sky from 210,000 years ago to 160,000 years ago, at about −1.8 in apparent magnitude. At −1.1, Aldebaran was brightest before this period; it and Capella were situated rather close to each other in the sky and approximated boreal pole stars at the time.

Distance


Capella is quite easy to find because it lies in a region of the sky particularly rich in exceptionally bright stars. Located about halfway between Polaris and Orion’s Belt, it is one of the six bright stars that form the Winter Circle, and the brightest of the stars that form Auriga’s conspicuous pentagon pattern. The small triangle of stars that form the Kids asterism makes Capella very easy to identify.

Some Information about Capella


What is special about Capella star?


In fact, Capella is the biggest and brightest yellow star in our sky. It's much bigger and brighter than our sun in absolute terms, and, of course, much farther away at about 42 light-years. That's in contrast to our sun's distance of 8 light-minutes.

What kind of star is Capella?


spectroscopic binary Capella, (Latin: “She-Goat”) also called Alpha Aurigae, sixth brightest star in the night sky and the brightest in the constellation Auriga, with an apparent visual magnitude of 0.08. Capella is a spectroscopic binary comprising two G-type giant stars that orbit each other every 104 days.

Why is Capella called the Goat star?


Capella is sometimes called the Goat Star because its name is derived from the diminutive of the Latin capra, meaning “female goat,” and means “the little goat.”
