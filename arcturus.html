ARCTURUS STAR

What is Arcturus Star?


Arcturus is the brightest star in the northern constellation of Boötes. With an apparent visual magnitude of −0.05, it is the third-brightest of the individual stars in the night sky, and the brightest in the northern celestial hemisphere.

Surface temperature: 4,290 K
Magnitude: -0.04
Radius: 17.671 million km
Distance to Earth: 36.66 light years
Constellation: Boötes
Spectral type: K1.5IIIFe-0.5
Coordinates: RA 14h 15m 40s | Dec +19° 10′ 56″

Observational History


Arcturus is actually the first star to have been observed (other than our Sun), during the daytime. This happened in 1635 when French astronomer Jean-Baptiste Morin, pointed his telescope and observed the star. Arcturus has also been seen at or just before sunset with the naked eye.
Arcturus is a red giant star in the Northern Hemisphere of Earth's sky and the brightest star in the constellation Boötes (the herdsman).

Some Information about Arcturus


What is special about the star Arcturus?


Image result for what is arcturus star The bright orange star Arcturus is especially noteworthy for its large proper motion, or sideways motion, on the dome of Earth's sky. Only Alpha Centauri – our sun's nearest neighbor among the stars – has a higher proper motion among the first-magnitude, or bright, stars in our stellar neighborhood.

What is Arcturus also known as?


Arcturus, also called Alpha Boötis, one of the five brightest stars in the night sky, and the brightest star in the northern constellation Boötes, with an apparent visual magnitude of −0.05. It is an orange-coloured giant star 36.7 light-years from Earth.

What does Arcturus mean star?


guardian or keeper of the bear Arcturus is Greek meaning 'guardian or keeper of the bear'. The Greeks saw this star as the guardian of Ursa Major and Ursa Minor, which are both nearby the constellation Boötes, where Arcturus is located.
